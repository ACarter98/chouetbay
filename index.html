About Chouet Bay

Chouet Bay reveals its magic as the day ends. While Guernsey's west coast gets sunset fame, locals know Chouet offers something special: an unobstructed view of the sun setting over the sea, framed by headlands on either side, with space to breathe.

The beach is wide and sandy, with gentle slopes making it safe for families. At low tide, it connects with neighbouring Grand Havre, creating a vast expanse of sand rarely crowded even in peak summer. The north coast location means it catches evening sunshine long after west coast beaches fall into shadow.

Rock Pool Paradise

The western end of the bay features rocky outcrops that create exceptional rock pooling at low tide. Children (and adults) can spend hours discovering crabs, fish, anemones, and starfish. The pools are particularly good after spring tides.

Evening Atmosphere

As sunset approaches, the atmosphere shifts. Dog walkers, photographers, and couples gather. The sun sinks slowly, painting everything gold. It's a daily show that never gets old, and Chouet provides front row seats.

Visitor Tips

  • Arrive 1 hour before sunset for best spot
  • Check tide times - beach is vast at low tide
  • Bring torch for rock pooling after dark
  • West side has best pools
  • Windy days create dramatic wave photos
  • Cobo Bay cafes 5 min drive for refreshments
